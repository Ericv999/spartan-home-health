## 4. FULFILLMENT ARCHITECTURE — HOW IT ACTUALLY WORKS

This is the critical section. Eric does NOT handle any of the medical or fulfillment process. Here is exactly how it flows:

### Customer Journey (what the customer experiences)
1. Customer visits spartanhomehealth.com
2. Selects His Plan, Her Plan, or Household Plan
3. Completes a 5-minute online health assessment/intake form (hosted by our white-label partner, branded as Spartan)
4. Pays via Stripe/credit card at checkout
5. A licensed provider from the partner network (OpenLoop or CareValidate) reviews the submission
6. If required by state law or clinical need, a video consultation is scheduled (typically within 2-3 days)
7. Provider makes independent prescribing decision — we have ZERO influence over this
8. If approved, lab work may be ordered (at-home kit shipped OR referral to local lab like Quest Diagnostics)
9. Lab results reviewed by provider, treatment plan finalized
10. Prescription sent to partner compounding pharmacy
11. Medication compounded, packaged in discreet temperature-controlled packaging
12. Shipped directly to customer's door (typically 3-9 days from approval)
13. Monthly refills: customer completes a refill form, pays monthly fee, medication ships automatically
14. Ongoing: provider check-ins, dosage adjustments, follow-up labs every 3-6 months
15. 24/7 messaging support available throughout

### What Spartan Home Health DOES in this flow
- Owns the brand, website, and marketing
- Drives customer acquisition (paid ads, content, SEO)
- Manages the checkout/payment flow (Stripe)
- Provides AI-powered customer service chatbot for non-clinical questions
- Monitors business analytics and performance
- Manages email/SMS communication flows (non-clinical)

### What the White-Label Partner DOES
- Provides the licensed clinician network (all 50 states)
- Handles the medical intake/assessment form (white-labeled to our brand)
- Manages provider-patient consultations (video or async)
- Writes prescriptions (independent clinical decision)
- Coordinates lab ordering and results
- Partners with compounding pharmacies for medication fulfillment
- Ships medication to customers
- Handles clinical customer support (medical questions go to clinicians)
- Manages all regulatory compliance (DEA, Ryan Haight Act, state telehealth laws, HIPAA)
- Handles credentialing, licensing, and provider oversight

### IMPORTANT: TRT is Schedule III
Testosterone is a Schedule III controlled substance under the DEA. This means:
- DEA compliance is required
- The Ryan Haight Act requires an in-person medical evaluation OR a valid telemedicine exception before prescribing
- As of late 2025, federal guidance has extended telehealth flexibilities for controlled substances
- Our white-label partner handles ALL of this compliance — we need to confirm their specific approach during onboarding
- This is the #1 question to ask both OpenLoop and CareValidate on the sales call

## 6. CERTIFICATIONS & COMPLIANCE

### LegitScript Healthcare Merchant Certification (CRITICAL — BLOCKING)
- **What:** Third-party certification required to run health/telehealth ads on Google, Meta, Microsoft, TikTok. Also required by Visa/Mastercard for card-not-present healthcare transactions.
- **Why:** Without LegitScript, we CANNOT run paid ads on any major platform. This is the #1 gatekeeper.
- **Timeline:** Typically 4-6 weeks. Can be fast-tracked to ~3 weeks through enterprise partners (CareValidate and OpenLoop are both LegitScript enterprise partners — ASK them to fast-track).
- **Process:** Create account → pay application fee → complete questionnaire → submit documentation (licenses, compliance proof, website review) → compliance verification → certification
- **Requirements:** Non-deceptive advertising, transparent pricing, accurate health information, proper disclaimers, state telehealth compliance, HIPAA compliance
- **Cost:** Varies — application fee + annual monitoring fee
- **Apply at:** legitscript.com/certification/telemedicine/
- **Action:** Ask BOTH OpenLoop and CareValidate on sales call if they can fast-track through their enterprise partnership

### Other Compliance Items
- **HIPAA:** Handled by white-label partner (their platform is HIPAA compliant)
- **State telehealth laws:** Handled by partner (some states require video consultation before prescribing)
- **DEA/Ryan Haight Act:** Handled by partner for Schedule III (testosterone)
- **Advertising compliance:** Our responsibility — no medical claims, no guarantees, proper disclaimers on all ads and website
- **FDA disclaimer:** Required on website — compounded medications are not FDA-approved
- **FTC compliance:** Truthful advertising, substantiated claims only

- State telehealth laws change frequently

### Platform Risk
- Ad accounts can be suspended (LegitScript helps but isn't bulletproof)
- **Mitigation:** Diversify channels. Build organic/content engine alongside paid. Email list is owned media.

### Partner Dependency
- Entirely dependent on OpenLoop/CareValidate for fulfillment
- **Mitigation:** Maintain relationships with multiple partners. Don't sign exclusive agreements if possible. Build brand equity that transcends any single partner.
